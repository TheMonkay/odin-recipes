Banh Boa


It is common to find these steamed buns being sold by vendors next to the street.
The cook will have a steamer next to their little cart cooking bahn boa. The allready
cooked banh boa will be on show in a warmed glass display case. The lucky comsumer has a choice
between the different sizes available.
The lucky client will then experience a pure bliss moment as the cloud soft sweet bun reveali
the savoury stuffing. A wide variety of villing are used and they include but are not limited
to pork, cabbage, mushrooms, onions and greens. Each banh boa hides a hidden treat, a quil egg.

The ingredients

For the dough

  1. All purpose flour (2 1/2 cups)- AP flour gives the bestresults for the bao texture
  2. Baking powder (1 tsp)
  3. Milk (2/3 cup)- Use whole milk.
  4. Instant yeast (1 tsp) - Combine with warm milk 10 min before use.
  5. Neutral oil(1 tbsp) - Canola oil works great.
  6. Granulated sugar (1 3/4) - White sugar to balance the savoury filling.

For the filling


  1. Ground pork (700 g)
  2. Oyster sauce (3 tbsp)
  3. Fish sauce (3 tbsp)
  4. Salt and pepper (to taste)
  5. Sugar (1 1/2 tsp)
  6. Shallots (2, Finely chopped)
  7. Green onions (2, chopped)
  8. Cornstarch (1 1/2 tbsp)
  9. Sesame oil (1 1/2 tbsp)
  10. Chinese sausage (Thinly sliced)
  11. Hard boiled eggs quartered or singular quail eggs (1 p. bun)

Method

Make the dough

  1. In a large bowl add the folloiwng in this order; oil, sugar, salt, baking powder, yeast and flour. Mix together, and then slowly add milk to the mixture. Do not over mix.
  2. Dust your hands with flour. Knead slightly and shape into a ball.
  3. Sprinkle flour on a large flat surface and knead the dough for 10 -15 min on the floured surface. Knead till smooth
  4. Coat a large ball with oil and place the dough inside. Cover with plastic wrap and leave to rise in a warm place for 30-60 minutes. Untill the dough has doubles in size.

Make the filling

  1. Combine all the ingrdients (except for the egg and chinese sausage). Mix well and let it marinade overnight.

Assembling the Banh Bao

  1. Gently devide the dough into 16 pieces once it has dubbled in size.
  2. Roll each ball into a flatten circle, with the middle slightly thicker.
  3. Place a scoop full of the pork filling in ball with an egg and chinese sausage.
  4. Wrap the filling in the dough, pinching and twisting the dough to make the classic shape.
  5. Placed the uncooked banh boa on a baking sheet and cover in plastic wrap to avoid drying.
  6. Let the bahn boa rest for 30 min in a warm place.

Cooking the bahn bao

  1. Bring steamer to boil. Place the uncooked bahn boa on pre cut parchment squares in the
    steamer (leave about 6 cm between each bun).
  2. Close steamer and let it cook for 12 minutes.
  3. Turn off heat and place buns on cooling rack, beast eaten immediatly. Can be stored fo up to
    3 days in the fridge.
